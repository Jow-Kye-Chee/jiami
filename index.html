our lab

The Advanced and Intelligent Energy Laboratory (AI-Energy Lab) is dedicated to advancing next-generation sustainable energy technologies. Its research focuses on multiphysics modeling, system-level control, optimization, and experimental validation of offshore wind and wave energy systems. The lab emphasizes integrating renewable energy with "blue economy" applications, including seawater desalination, aquaculture, autonomous underwater vehicle charging, and marine carbon dioxide removal. By bridging vibration control, energy harvesting, robotics, and autonomous control, the lab aims to translate fundamental research into practical engineering solutions, thereby enhancing the efficiency, reliability, and scalability of renewable energy utilization.

Marine Carbon Capture and Storage

Our Marine Carbon Capture and Storage project focuses on developing innovative technologies for capturing and storing carbon dioxide in marine environments. This research combines renewable energy systems with advanced carbon sequestration methods to address critical climate change challenges. The project explores the integration of offshore renewable energy infrastructure with carbon capture technologies, creating synergistic systems that not only generate clean energy but also actively remove CO2 from the atmosphere. Our approach includes the development of efficient capture mechanisms, safe storage solutions in marine environments, and comprehensive monitoring systems to ensure environmental safety and effectiveness. This interdisciplinary research bridges ocean engineering, environmental science, and energy systems to create practical solutions for climate mitigation.

Prof. Mi and team members presented their latest research on "Intelligent Control Strategies for Offshore Wind Energy Systems" at the IEEE OCEANS 2024 conference in Singapore, receiving positive feedback from the international marine energy community.
