our lab

The Advanced and Intelligent Energy Laboratory (AI-Energy Lab) is dedicated to advancing next-generation sustainable energy technologies. Its research focuses on multiphysics modeling, system-level control, optimization, and experimental validation of offshore wind and wave energy systems. The lab emphasizes integrating renewable energy with "blue economy" applications, including seawater desalination, aquaculture, autonomous underwater vehicle charging, and marine carbon dioxide removal. By bridging vibration control, energy harvesting, robotics, and autonomous control, the lab aims to translate fundamental research into practical engineering solutions, thereby enhancing the efficiency, reliability, and scalability of renewable energy utilization.

projects

Offshore Wind Energy Systems

Advanced modeling and control strategies for offshore wind turbines, focusing on multiphysics simulation and real-time optimization to maximize energy capture while ensuring structural integrity in harsh marine environments.

Wave Energy Conversion

Innovative wave energy converter designs with intelligent control systems for optimal power extraction from ocean waves, integrating advanced sensors and adaptive algorithms for varying sea conditions.

Blue Economy Integration

Integration of renewable energy systems with marine applications including seawater desalination, aquaculture systems, and autonomous underwater vehicle charging stations for sustainable ocean operations.

news&updates

Conference Presentation at IEEE OCEANS 2024

March 15, 2024

Prof. Mi and team members presented their latest research on "Intelligent Control Strategies for Offshore Wind Energy Systems" at the IEEE OCEANS 2024 conference in Singapore, receiving positive feedback from the international marine energy community.

NSF Grant Awarded for Wave Energy Research

February 28, 2024

The AI-Energy Lab has been awarded a $500,000 NSF grant to develop next-generation wave energy conversion technologies. This funding will support our research on adaptive control systems for wave energy converters.

New Publication in Renewable Energy Journal

January 15, 2024

Our paper "Multiphysics Modeling of Offshore Wind-Wave Hybrid Energy Systems" has been published in the prestigious Renewable Energy journal, showcasing our innovative approach to integrated marine energy systems.
